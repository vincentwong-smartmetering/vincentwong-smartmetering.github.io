Key Takeaways

  • Many utility challenges stem from limited operational visibility rather than infrastructure alone.
  • Smart metering enables near real-time visibility compared with traditional monthly meter reading approaches.
  • Smart metering should be viewed as an end-to-end system involving meters, communications, HES, MDMS, and analytics.
  • Utility digitalization is a progressive journey from mechanical metering to AMR, AMI, and digitally enabled operations.
  • Total cost of ownership extends beyond hardware to include communications, operations, maintenance, and lifecycle management.
  • Successful deployments depend on technology, integration, stakeholder alignment, and field execution.

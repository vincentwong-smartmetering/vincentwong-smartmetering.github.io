Smart Metering for Asia: Enabling Digital Water Management

Role: Speaker

Presented at AsiaWater 2026, this session explored how smart metering and digital technologies are helping water utilities improve operational visibility, reduce Non-Revenue Water (NRW), and accelerate digital transformation.

A key theme of the session was that smart metering should be viewed as an end-to-end system rather than a standalone device. Real value is created through the integration of meters, communication networks, Head-End Systems (HES), Meter Data Management Systems (MDMS), and analytics that convert data into operational decisions.

The discussion also highlighted that utility digitalization is a progressive journey, typically evolving from mechanical metering to AMR, AMI, and ultimately digitally enabled utility operations. Each stage introduces new technical, operational, and organizational challenges that must be managed carefully.

Additional topics included lifecycle management, total cost of ownership, system integration, stakeholder alignment, field deployment considerations, and the critical success factors for large-scale smart metering programs.

The session concluded that the water industry is increasingly moving beyond technology deployment towards realizing measurable operational impact through data-driven decision making and digital water management practices.
